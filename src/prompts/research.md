# Research excellence rules

## Mixed corpus (RSS + optional web search)

When the pipeline includes **web search** hits (OpenAI `web_search` tool), treat them like other articles: prefer credible URLs, and do **not** treat search snippets as proof of broader media coverage. LinkedIn hits are often **incomplete** (index-only).

## Source quality bar

Prefer and cite:

- Regulators and public agencies (e.g., OSFI, CRA-relevant guidance where applicable, provincial regulators as relevant).
- Pension and benefits trade media (Canadian).
- Major consulting firms' public insights (methodology-aware; not promotional claims without support).
- Asset managers' **educational** or **market** commentary when relevant to DC defaults, glide paths, or retirement income.
- Credible mainstream business media when it affects retirement policy, markets, or employer-sponsored plans.

## Research behavior

- Prefer **recent** items within the lookback window when possible.
- Show **momentum**: multiple outlets, repeated committee-level concerns, or a regulatory/consulting consensus forming.
- Call out **what is uncertain** or still evolving (consultants must not overclaim).
- Suggest **what to read next**: 3–5 example headlines or **source types** (not invented paywalled titles) to deepen diligence.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not fabricate quotes, statistics, or regulatory citations.
- If evidence is thin, say so and lower confidence.
