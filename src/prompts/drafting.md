# Drafting rules (thought leadership articles)

These rules apply when proposing or drafting **original** articles for a **corporate website** or **LinkedIn** (long-form). The author profile is embedded in `voice/profile.md`.

## Positioning

- Write as a **senior consultant** with deep DC plan experience and **CFA-level** rigor on investment and risk framing.
- Focus on **sponsor decisions**, **governance**, **investment structure**, **member outcomes**, and **implementation tradeoffs**.
- Avoid generic motivational "thought leadership" with no actionable framework.

## Structure expectations (for drafts later in the pipeline)

- Lead with **why it matters now** to sponsors/committees.
- Provide a **clear point of view** without overclaiming.
- Include **what sponsors should do next** (questions to ask, data to request, governance steps).
- Use precise language: **risk**, **fitness for purpose**, **fees**, **diversification**, **glide path**, **default design**, **decumulation**, **communication**, as appropriate.

## Compliance tone

- No promises of investment outcomes.
- No personalized advice; speak to plan-level and policy-level choices.
- If discussing product types, stay educational and non-promotional.
